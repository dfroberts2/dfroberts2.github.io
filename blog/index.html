About This Blog

The purpose of this blog is to track my progress through DevBootcamp. Going into DevBootcamp, I've had a limited exposure to programming and will be building off of that knowledge with the goal of becoming a web developer. Before DevBootcamp, I only had experience with the following topics:

  • UNIX command line
  • HTML
  • Matlab
  • C

So it is clear to see that my technical knowledge is not at 0, but that I have a lot to learn. I will be discussing here the new ideas I've learned and how they build on my own knowledge. I'm most looking forward to learning how to write Ruby and use SQL. My previous work as an analyst used large data resources and we would be in communication with backend developers who managed the data systems. The work they do always fascinated me, and I hope to be capable of doing the same by the end of the DevBootcamp, or sometime in the near future.

To finish up, the internet and the software development world has always been on a rapid pace and it is important to have a record of those changes. Technical blogs are useful to both those reflecting, and others who may wish to learn more. Enjoy.
